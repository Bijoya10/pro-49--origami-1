Camel

  1. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  2. There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.

Chameleon

  1. They mainly change color in order to communicate or regulate body temperature.
  2. Skin crystals enable them to change color at will.

Pigeon

  1. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals. They are one of only a small number of species to pass the ‘mirror test’ – a test of self recognition.
  2. Pigeons are highly sociable animals. They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.

Teddy-Bear

  1. The teddy was named after President Theodore Roosevelt, after he refused to shoot a bear during a 1902 hunting trip.
  2. spired by a cartoon featuring the event, shopkeeper Morris Michtom and his wife Rose made a stuffed bear with a sign 'Teddy's bear' and left it in their shop window.

Panda

  1. The giant panda's distinct black-and-white markings have two functions: camouflage and communication. Most of the panda - its face, neck, belly, rump - is white to help it hide in snowy habitats
  2. Like domestic cats, giant pandas have vertical slits for pupils.

Flying-Cicada

  1. Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs. They are about the size of a grain of rice when they drop from a tree branch to the ground and start digging.
  2. They’re true bugs (from the order Hemiptera). So you can call them a bug and be scientifically accurate.
